How Global Warming Can Affect Winter?


Simple, the warming of the planet disturbs atmospheric currents, which leads to a tendency to intensify some events, be they extreme heat waves or cold ones.


How does a harsher winter affect the population of theoretically warmer regions?


Taking as an example the intense cold front that hit Texas in 2021, in mid-February, residents were without power for weeks, there were more than 40 dead from the cold, as they died from hypothermia and in extreme cases, due to lack of electricity, residents had to light fires inside their homes to keep warm, which caused many house fires.

How Global Warming Can Affect Fire Risk?

Climate change has led to numerous environmental changes that can increase the frequency and magnitude of dangerous fire weather – increased drought, high air temperatures, low humidity, dry lightning and high winds, resulting in more droughts and longer fire seasons. The increasing frequency and magnitude of hazardous weather conditions is causing vegetation that would not normally burn (eg tropical forests, permafrost and peat bogs) to dry out and ignite.
